Common Mental Health Issues

Learn about the most common mental health issues, their symptoms, and how to manage them. Our resources provide valuable information and support to help you understand and cope with these challenges.

Disclaimer: This website provides informational content and is not a substitute for professional medical or mental health advice. For help, please consult a licensed professional. In emergencies, contact emergency services.

Anxiety

Understand the signs, symptoms, and treatments for anxiety disorders.

Anxiety disorders involve excessive fear, worry, or nervousness that can disrupt daily activities. It is a common mental health issue, with various forms such as generalized anxiety disorder (GAD), social anxiety, panic disorder, and phobias.

Common symptoms of anxiety disorders include:

  • Excessive worry or fear
  • Restlessness or irritability
  • Difficulty concentrating
  • Physical symptoms like rapid heartbeat, sweating, or trembling
  • Sleep disturbances

Various treatment options are available for managing anxiety disorders, including:

  • Therapy (e.g., cognitive-behavioral therapy)
  • Medications (e.g., antidepressants, anti-anxiety medications)
  • Lifestyle changes (e.g., stress management, exercise, relaxation techniques)
  • Support groups and self-help strategies

Common symptoms of depression include:

  • Persistent feelings of sadness or emptiness
  • Loss of interest in activities once enjoyed
  • Changes in appetite or weight
  • Difficulty sleeping or oversleeping
  • Feelings of worthlessness or guilt
  • Difficulty concentrating or making decisions
  • Thoughts of death

Various treatment options are available for managing depression, including:

  • Therapy (e.g., cognitive-behavioral therapy, interpersonal therapy)
  • Medications (e.g., antidepressants)
  • Lifestyle changes (e.g., exercise, healthy diet, sleep hygiene)
  • Support groups and self-help strategies
Eating Disorders

Discover resources and support for managing eating disorders.

Eating disorders are serious mental health conditions characterized by unhealthy relationships with food, body image, and weight. Common types include anorexia nervosa, bulimia nervosa, and binge-eating disorder.

Common signs and symptoms of eating disorders include:

  • Restricting food intake or avoiding certain foods
  • Binge eating or eating large amounts of food in a short period
  • Engaging in purging behaviors (e.g., vomiting, using laxatives)
  • Excessive exercise or preoccupation with body weight
  • Distorted body image or dissatisfaction with body shape
  • Physical symptoms like fatigue, dizziness, or hair loss

Various treatment options are available for managing eating disorders, including:

  • Therapy (e.g., cognitive-behavioral therapy, family-based therapy)
  • Medical monitoring and nutritional counseling
  • Medications (e.g., antidepressants for co-occurring conditions)
  • Support groups and self-help strategies
Trauma

Learn about trauma and ways to heal from traumatic experiences.

Trauma results from experiencing or witnessing a deeply distressing event, such as abuse, violence, or accidents. Post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD) is a common condition associated with trauma.

Common symptoms of trauma and PTSD include:

  • Flashbacks or intrusive memories of the traumatic event
  • Emotional numbness or avoidance of reminders of the trauma
  • Hyperarousal (e.g., irritability, difficulty sleeping)
  • Negative changes in mood or beliefs about oneself or others
  • Difficulty concentrating or feeling on edge

Various treatment options are available for managing trauma and PTSD, including:

  • Therapy (e.g., cognitive-behavioral therapy, exposure therapy)
  • Medications (e.g., antidepressants, anti-anxiety medications)
  • Eye movement desensitization and reprocessing (EMDR)
  • Support groups and self-help strategies
Relationship Issues

Get advice on managing and improving relationships.

Relationship issues can involve difficulties in family dynamics, friendships, or romantic partnerships. They may stem from communication issues, trust issues, or differing values and goals.

Common relationship challenges include:

  • Communication problems
  • Trust issues
  • Conflicts over finances, parenting, or other issues
  • Infidelity or betrayal
  • Differing values, goals, or expectations

Various support options are available for managing relationship issues, including:

  • Couples therapy or family therapy
  • Individual therapy to address personal issues impacting relationships
  • Communication skills training
  • Mediation or conflict resolution strategies
  • Support groups or workshops
Addiction

Access resources for overcoming addiction and finding support.

Addiction is a chronic disorder characterized by compulsive substance use or behaviors despite harmful consequences. It can affect mental, emotional, and physical health and relationships.

Common signs and symptoms of addiction include:

  • Loss of control over substance use or behavior
  • Preoccupation with obtaining and using substances
  • Continued use despite negative consequences
  • Withdrawal symptoms when not using the substance
  • Changes in mood, behavior, or relationships

Various treatment options are available for managing addiction, including:

  • Therapy (e.g., cognitive-behavioral therapy, motivational interviewing)
  • Medications (e.g., opioid agonists, nicotine replacement therapy)
  • Support groups (e.g., 12-step programs)
  • Residential treatment programs and detoxification
